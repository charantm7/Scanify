## 1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website or use a web application. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide information to site owners.

Cookies are not viruses or harmful programs. They cannot access other files on your device, run programs, or execute malicious code. They simply store small pieces of information that help the website function as intended.

Similar technologies include:

- **Local Storage:** Browser-based storage used for session state and preferences
- **Session Storage:** Temporary data storage cleared when the browser tab is closed
- **Pixel tags / Web beacons:** Tiny invisible images used to track page visits and email opens
- **Device fingerprinting:** A technique that identifies devices based on configuration (we use this only for security purposes)

For simplicity, this policy refers to all such technologies collectively as **"cookies."**

## 2. Why Scanify Uses Cookies

Scanify uses cookies to:

- Keep you logged into your Restaurant Partner account across sessions
- Remember your preferences and settings within the platform
- Ensure the platform functions correctly and securely
- Understand how users interact with the platform so we can improve it
- Detect and prevent fraudulent or abusive activity
- Measure the performance and reach of our platform

We do **not** use cookies to:

- Serve you third-party advertising
- Sell your browsing data to advertisers
- Track your activity across unrelated websites

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

### 3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

**Required for the platform to function. Cannot be disabled.**

These cookies are essential to provide you with the services you have requested. Without them, the platform cannot function. They include:

| Cookie Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `session_id` | Maintains your login session as a Restaurant Partner | Session (cleared on logout) |
| `csrf_token` | Protects against cross-site request forgery attacks | Session |
| `auth_token` | Authenticates your account securely | 7 days |

### 3.2 Functional Cookies

**Enhance your experience. Disabled by default — enabled with your consent.**

These cookies remember your preferences to provide a more personalised experience:

| Cookie Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `lang_pref` | Remembers your preferred display language | 1 year |
| `theme_pref` | Remembers light/dark mode preference | 1 year |
| `dashboard_state` | Remembers your last dashboard view/filter settings | 30 days |

### 3.3 Analytics Cookies

**Help us understand usage. Disabled by default — enabled with your consent.**

These cookies collect anonymised information about how users interact with our platform. All data is aggregated and does not identify you personally:

| Cookie Name | Provider | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| `_ga` | Google Analytics | Distinguishes unique visitors | 2 years |
| `_ga_*` | Google Analytics | Maintains session state | 2 years |
| `_scanify_analytics` | Scanify (first-party) | Tracks feature usage and page views | 13 months |

### 3.4 Security Cookies

**Required for fraud prevention. Cannot be disabled.**

These cookies are used to protect the platform and its users from fraudulent access and abuse:

| Cookie Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `fp_id` | Device fingerprint for anomaly detection | 30 days |
| `rate_limit_token` | Prevents API abuse and brute-force attacks | Session |

## 6. Managing and Disabling Cookies

### 6.1 Through Scanify's Cookie Settings

When you first visit the Scanify platform, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner where you can accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can update your preferences at any time via the **Cookie Settings** link in the footer of our website.

**Note:** Disabling strictly necessary or security cookies will prevent you from using core platform features such as logging in.

### 6.2 Through Your Browser

You can also manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

- View and delete existing cookies
- Block all cookies or block cookies from specific sites
- Set preferences for accepting or rejecting cookies from specific categories

Browser-specific instructions:

- **Google Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
- **Mozilla Firefox:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
- **Safari:** Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
- **Microsoft Edge:** Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

**Note:** Blocking all cookies may break certain website features and functionality across many websites you visit, not just Scanify.

### 6.3 Opting Out of Analytics

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking specifically, you can use the [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).
